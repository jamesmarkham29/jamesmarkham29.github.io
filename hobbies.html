Bass Guitar

The bass guitar is a stringed instrument that provides the low-end rhythm and harmony in most modern music. Typically with four strings tuned an octave lower than a standard guitar, it anchors the groove alongside drums. The electric bass guitar was developed in the 1950s, most notably by Leo Fender, whose Precision Bass became the first widely adopted model. Since then, the bass has been central to genres ranging from rock and funk to jazz and pop.

Running

Running is a form of locomotion and exercise where a person moves rapidly on foot, faster than walking, by propelling themselves forward in strides. It is one of the most natural human activities, practiced for survival, travel, and sport throughout history. Organized running as a competitive sport dates back to the ancient Olympic Games in Greece around 776 BCE, where footraces were among the earliest events. Today, running is both a recreational activity and a global sport, with marathons and track events drawing millions of participants.

Weight Lifting
